How to Recognize a Tufted Titmouse

- Gray head and back

- White chest and belly

- Hint of light brown under wings

- Gray tuft on head (sometimes not seen)

- Small black patch above beak

Description from Cornell Lab of Ornithology:

A little gray bird with an echoing voice, the Tufted Titmouse is common in eastern deciduous forests and a frequent visitor to feeders. The large black eyes, small, round bill, and brushy crest gives these birds a quiet but eager expression that matches the way they flit through canopies, hang from twig-ends, and drop in to bird feeders.
