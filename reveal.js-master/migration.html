The Atlantic American Flyway

The Atlantic Americas Flyway connects the Canadian Arctic Archipelago to South Americaâ€™s southern tip, Tierra del Fuego. Numerous arctic breeding birds move south along the flyway for the northern winter, in addition to hundreds of other species, who travel north to breed.

The Atlantic American Flyway hosts over 250 bird species and serves as an important resting and feeding site for migrating and wintering waterfowl, including more than 35,000 Canada geese and 15,000 ducks using the Atlantic Flyway during the fall migration. Warblers, which travel along this flyway, come from South America, through the US to Canada, where they breed. Most birds make this 1,500-2,000 mile journey in about two or three days, never stopping to rest or eat.
